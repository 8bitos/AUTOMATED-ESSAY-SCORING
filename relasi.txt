Table Explanations (Automatic Essay Grading Focus)

users:
Stores people who interact with the grading system, including students who submit essays and teachers who review or finalize scores.

materials:
Stores learning materials that provide the context for essay questions.

modules:
Stores uploaded module files tied to a material (optional structure for organizing content).

essay_questions:
Stores the essay prompts, grading configuration (keywords, ideal answer, weight), and optional rubric data.

rubrics:
Stores detailed scoring criteria per essay question, including aspect descriptors and weights.

essay_submissions:
Stores student answers and the AI grading lifecycle fields (status, timestamps, error info, attempt count).

ai_results:
Stores AI-generated scores, feedback, and raw model responses for each submission.

teacher_reviews:
Stores teacher overrides or confirmations of AI results, including revised scores and feedback.

ai_api_usage_logs:
Stores audit information about AI API calls for monitoring, analytics, and cost tracking.

ai_grading_cache:
Stores cached AI grading responses to avoid reprocessing identical requests.


Relation Explanations (Why the links exist)

modules.material_id -> materials.id:
Each module belongs to a specific material.

essay_questions.material_id -> materials.id:
Each question is tied to a material so the prompt is anchored to its learning context.

essay_questions.module_id -> modules.id:
Optional link when a question is specifically part of a module.

rubrics.question_id -> essay_questions.id:
Rubrics define the scoring criteria for a specific question.

essay_submissions.soal_id -> essay_questions.id:
A submission answers one specific essay question.

essay_submissions.siswa_id -> users.id:
The user who submitted the answer (student).

ai_results.submission_id -> essay_submissions.id:
AI grading results are generated for a single submission.

teacher_reviews.submission_id -> essay_submissions.id:
Teacher review is attached to a single submission for final grading.

teacher_reviews.teacher_id -> users.id:
Identifies the teacher who reviewed or adjusted the score.
